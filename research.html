My research

Theoretical particle physics - phenomenology

Disentangling "Beyond the Standard Model“ events, expected to be rare, requires theoretical input and in particular very precise calculations. The core formalism for this is the evaluation of Feynman graphs in a perturbative expansion in the coupling strength of the particle interactions. High order results are now absolutely necessary to meet the precision demanded by experiments. To achieve this involves evaluating Feynman integrals whose number increases exponentially with coupling constant order. The underlying techniques and algorithms to achieve this are data intensive and require significant computer management.

My research focouses on the computation of loop corrections to physical processes. I work on the weak sector of the Standard Model, in particular leptonic and semi-leptonic decays I am also nterested in dimensional and non-dimensional regularisation methods and their impact on the calculations.

PhD project

I am currently working on the computation of 2 loop corrections for leptonic and semi-leptonic decays. I use FeynArts to generate the diagrams, and wrote my own packages in Mathematica to simplify the amplitudes.

Non-dimensional regularisation methods

In my masters project, I worked on the computation of 1-loop corrections to the decay of the Higgs boson into gluonsm modelled by an EFT theory. I made use of a non-dimensional regularisation method - Implicit Regularisation - to treat the divergences, checking the consistensy between this method and the Kinoshita–Lee–Nauenberg theorem. You find out more in my master thesis and in the arXiv.
